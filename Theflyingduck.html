The Flying Duck like a lot of our other venues suggested on our website, is another hidden gem which this time is hidden underground in Glasgow (source 1). At first glance this venue does not look like much, however, as you go further down the tunnel into the heart of the Flying Duck, it turns into one of Glasgowâ€™s best bars, restaurants, late night ventures and even has enough space to provide areas for local gigs and events. Since 2007, people have been visiting this underground tunnel both for its late-night events, board games, weekly pub quiz and their popular vegan dishes (source 3). Similar, to its sister bars Mono, Stereo, The Old Hairdressers and The 78, the Flying Duck has ensured to keep up with the theme in its sister bars by creating a shabby-chic and rustic feeling throughout it. This has been a result of the numerous pieces of vintage furniture that are scattered about the bar and the theme of layout of the entire bar has a vintage kitchen look about it (source 2).

Upon visiting the venue, if you have visited any of its sister bars such as Mono, The Flying Duck and The 78, then you will notice that they all are very similar in style (source 3). This style we talk about has been obtained by stripping back the building to reveal its original features which gives it a rustic and industrial like appearance (source 1). Once you pass the ground floor and make your way up the study staircase the venue transforms into a large extensive exhibition space which can be used for a variety of different events such as exhibitions, gigs, theatre shows and can even be hired out for a club night (source 1).
