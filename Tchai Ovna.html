Tchai Ovna is located at the end of a side cobbled street, but despite its hidden spot it is one of the most renowned tea shops. This space used to be a 19th-century stables, whereas today it offers a large variety of teas from all over the world as well as vegetarian food in a cosy, dimly lit and beautifully decorated room. (Source)

Tchai Ovna also hosts live music nights, during which one can enjoy a warm cup of tea with biscuits. The tea shop also supports local artists: it exhibits the artworks of a different local artist each month, and the images are spread across the room for everyone to see. To have their works exhibited, one only needs to get in contact with the owner of the shop and send their art.

G5G recommends:
Michael Simons (Mondays, 8pm - 10pm - Michael Simons is a blues musician who plays the guitar and sings inspired by Bob Dylan, Stephan Grossman and others. He usually brings other talented friends to play with him and his show has happened in Tchai Ovna for a number of months, every Monday. His music can be listened to on Tchai Ovna website too, for those who have not had the chance to go yet.)
