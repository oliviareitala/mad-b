Kinning Park Complex is an independent community centre just off the Kinning Park Subway station. It is run by locals since 1996 and also develops community art projects around the area. Their objectives for 2016/2017 state that they want to “Improve the building and secure future use”, “Support staff to make KPC a great place to work, play and create”, “Increase community engagement with the KPC”, “Deliver more activity” and “Generate more revenue”. (Source)

One of Kinning Park Complex’ current projects is the Waste Not Want initiative, which aims to make KPC a zero waste building. How they pursue this target is for instance through the KPCafé which serves and prepares dishes with surplus food from chains such as Tesco and Marks & Spencer. They also organise eco-friendly workshops that for instance teach skills in “up-cycling” (reusing old materials in innovative ways to create enhanced new products) and other crafts. (Source)

To achieve these goals, KPC supports new projects and engages with the local community in order to enable the vibrant nature of the complex and its community. The spaces are used for various purposes from sport and dance to theatre, film, conferences and family events.
