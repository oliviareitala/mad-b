Mackintosh Queen’s Cross is a venue for music concerts, seminars, workshops, weddings and functions, designed by Charles Rennie Mackintosh. Commissioned by the Free Church in 1896, the church is the only one of its kind in the world, incorporating the Mackintosh Gothic style into traditional religious architecture (1).

The church came into possession of the Charles Rennie Mackintosh (CRM) Society after its purpose as a church hall fell into disuse, and has since been renovated into an exciting and illuminating museum space, exhibiting an array of Mackintosh design and regional history throughout its halls (2).
