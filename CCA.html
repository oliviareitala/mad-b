Centre for Contemporary Arts is in Glasgow and is one of the main contemporary creative art galleries in Europe (2). The actual building itself is steeped in history as it was previously known as the Third Eye Centre (1975-1991) which was also an arts centre founded by Tom McGrath in 1974 (3). This long history has enabled the CCA to become a key role in the cultural life of the city and contribute massively Glasgows vibrant art scene. Throughout the year they have various exciting programmes on offer which the majority of will be free to enter (1). In fact, in 2016-17, CCA successfully participated with and worked with 1,075 events and 26 festivals (4). The works involved with these programmes tend to take many forms, such as art exhibitions, film, music, public speaking, festivals and many more (1).

G5G recommends:
Short films for creatives with Lesley Traynor (4) (10th April, 7pm, The Scottish writers centre presents an event for creative minds to come together and discuss their work with professional Lesley Traynor and will be guided through the process of turning their creative work into a short film. In addition to this those in attendance will have the opportunity to read out some of their creative writing to the audience along with film and music, in order to provide them with the experience of using this method).
